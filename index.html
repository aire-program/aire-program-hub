Applied AI Innovation & Research Enablement (AIRE) Program

“The Applied AI Innovation and Research Enablement (AIRE) Program is a coordinated initiative in the College of Social Science at Michigan State University. It supports responsible and effective use of artificial intelligence across teaching, research, and academic operations by providing a connected set of learning, experimentation, and analytics resources.”

“AIRE brings together AI literacy development, research experimentation, and institutional insight in one program. It gives faculty, staff, students, and leadership practical ways to explore AI, test workflows, and make informed decisions based on evidence.”

“Originally developed on internal MSU systems and later mirrored to GitHub with synthetic data, AIRE demonstrates how an academic unit can build an AI enablement ecosystem that is both well-governed and adaptable. The public mirror allows other institutions to examine the program’s structure and workflows without exposing confidential information.”

Program Structure

Four Coordinated Pillars

AIRE consists of four coordinated components that work together to support learning design, research workflows, personalized feedback, and analytics-informed planning.

Pillar One

AIRE Literacy Hub

The AIRE Literacy Hub provides tutorials, examples, and governance-aligned guidance that help faculty, staff, students, and researchers build foundational AI skills using widely available tools. It is designed to support teaching, advising, research assistance, and student-focused work.

Explore

Pillar Two

AIRE Researcher Sandbox

The AIRE Researcher Sandbox is a reproducible environment where researchers and graduate students trial AI-enabled workflows. Colab guides cover text preparation, embeddings, retrieval-augmented generation, evaluation methods, and adaptation of workflows to discipline-specific projects.

Explore

Pillar Three

AIRE Impact Dashboard

The AIRE Impact Dashboard provides an institutional analytics view using synthetic data to illustrate participation, confidence, and department-level readiness. It mirrors the internal analytic structure so usage patterns and readiness indicators can be reviewed without exposing institutional records.

Explore

Pillar Four

AIRE Personalization Layer

The AIRE Personalization Layer includes the Micro Tutor and Learner Scorecard, providing prompt-level coaching and learner-facing progress views. Feedback and telemetry align with the Impact Dashboard, connecting individual guidance to program-level readiness analysis.

Practice & Use

How AIRE Is Used Across the College

“In practice, AIRE supports different kinds of work across the college. Instructors use the Literacy Hub to introduce responsible AI activities into courses and advising. Researchers and graduate students rely on the Sandbox to test workflows before applying them to funded projects. Leadership teams consult the Impact Dashboard to understand participation patterns and areas where focused support may be helpful.”

“The shared framework across components makes it easier to connect learning activities, research workflows, and institutional decision making. Indicators from the dashboard help inform the design of new workshops and Sandbox modules. Feedback collected through the Micro Tutor and Scorecard contributes to the development of future learning materials. This creates a cycle of learning, experimentation, and improvement that can adjust as needs change.”

Learning, Experimentation, and Insight

“AIRE functions as a continuous cycle. Individuals and departments begin with the Literacy Hub. Research teams experiment in the Sandbox. Leadership examines readiness and participation in the Impact Dashboard. Insights guide updates to training, workflows, and support structures.”

Leadership & Administration

“Leadership teams, department chairs, and program directors use the Impact Dashboard to understand participation, confidence changes, and departmental readiness. The dashboard supports planning discussions by illustrating where AI practices are emerging and where adoption may be uneven.”

Researchers & Graduate Students

“Researchers and graduate students use the Researcher Sandbox to explore AI-enabled workflows in a reproducible environment. Workflows demonstrate complete pipelines that can be adapted to discipline-specific research questions.”

Faculty, Staff & Students

“Faculty, staff, and students use the Literacy Hub to access tutorials and examples that introduce AI in practical and responsible ways. The Hub supports exploratory learning, course integration, and ongoing professional development.”

Governance

Governance, Data, and Responsible AI

“AIRE treats governance and data stewardship as central program elements. Internal deployments rely on college data under existing institutional policies. Public mirrors use synthetic datasets so that program structure and methods can be shared without exposing confidential information.”

“Across all components, the program emphasizes:”

  • clear documentation of how AI tools are used in teaching, research, and operations
  • attention to privacy, consent, and data minimization in internal workflows
  • transparent use of synthetic data in public examples and demonstrations
  • reproducible methods that can be reviewed, adapted, and audited
  • alignment with institutional values around equity, accessibility, and inclusion
  • ongoing evaluation of learning impact and readiness to inform future program design

Program-Level Safeguards

“The public version of AIRE demonstrates how a program can balance transparency with responsible data handling. Synthetic datasets make the public mirrors possible while internal workflows remain aligned with established review processes.”

Code & Resources

Public Access Points

These public repositories mirror the internal AIRE infrastructure, using synthetic data to provide transparent and practical entry points to each component.

  1. AIRE Literacy Hub — https://applied-ai-literacy.github.io/hub/ — tutorials and teaching-ready materials that support faculty, staff, students, and advisors.
  2. AIRE Researcher Sandbox — https://github.com/aire-program/aire-researcher-sandbox — reproducible notebooks for AI-enabled research workflows and evaluation methods.
  3. AIRE Impact Dashboard — https://github.com/aire-program/aire-impact-dashboard — synthetic-data dashboard showing participation, confidence, and readiness indicators.
  4. AIRE Learner Scorecard — https://github.com/aire-program/aire-learner-scorecard — Micro Tutor and Scorecard tools for prompt-level guidance and learner-facing analytics.

These repositories mirror the internal AIRE systems, substitute synthetic datasets for institutional data, and support institutions and teams examining program structure or adapting components.
